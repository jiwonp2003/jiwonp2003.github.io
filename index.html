ATL-Trees: Forest Age Mapping — City of Atlanta

GIS Deep Learning Python
Urban Forestry  ·  Geospatial AI  ·  Temporal Analysis

Built an end-to-end geospatial deep learning pipeline to map and track Atlanta's urban forest across eight decades of historical aerial imagery (1938–1978). The system automatically classifies every pixel as forest or non-forest and then traces each stand's history to determine whether it is legacy forest, regrown forest, or cleared land — supporting the City of Atlanta's land acquisition and ecological decision-making.

What I Built

  • Preprocessing: Reprojected historical mosaics to EPSG:26916 via WarpedVRT, converted multi-band imagery to grayscale (BT.601 luminance), and extracted 256×256 chips with per-tile 2nd–98th percentile normalization. Implemented polygon-level 70/15/15 train/val/test splits to prevent data leakage.
  • Model: Fine-tuned a DeepLabV3 + ResNet-50 (ImageNet pretrained) for binary segmentation (forest / non-forest). Used WeightedRandomSampler and random augmentations to address class imbalance; trained with fp16 mixed precision for ~1.7× speedup on GPU.
  • Inference: Chunk-based wall-to-wall inference (4096×4096 px blocks) with 50% overlapping tiles merged via softmax probability accumulation, keeping RAM usage bounded across large city-wide rasters.
  • Temporal tracking: Pixel-by-pixel comparison across all year mosaics to classify legacy forest (never disturbed), young forest (regrown after clearing), and non-forest. Detected hidden regrowth gaps with a configurable year threshold.
  • Diagnostics: Wrote a debug_pipeline.py with 10-point automated checks covering CRS alignment, spatial overlap, label mask sanity, NoData contamination, and chip count validation.
